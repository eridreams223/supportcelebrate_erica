Chapter One

The Degree & the Dream 🎓

On the 25th of March, I received my second Master's degree — a Master of Science in Reproductive and Sexual Health Research, with Merit, from the London School of Hygiene & Tropical Medicine.

It has been a dream of mine for nearly a decade to earn a public health degree. I finally did it.

⭐ Special note: I received a distinction on my summer project! The manuscript is now in the final stages for publication!

I've said time and time again that my year at LSHTM was life-changing and one of the best years of my life. London has floored me. This decision gifted me in ways I could never have comprehended, and I do not overlook the immense privilege of having experiences that people only dream of.

I've come to find London as home for the foreseeable future and moved two weeks ago to a new flat with my dear friend Geneva, who I met in the programme. We are excited for what this new post-grad season holds. The public health world is navigating a complicated moment globally, but we see plenty of opportunity to insert ourselves into the space with our newfound knowledge.

Chapter Two

An Unexpected Recovery Journey 🌿

Since December, I've been navigating a challenging respiratory illness and what we now understand to be an underlying asthma exacerbation triggered by my (very sensitive) allergies. It's been a humbling season — everything from walking to commuting flares my symptoms, and I've spent most of my time at home managing this carefully.

It's hard to believe that nearly a third of a year has passed and I'm still navigating this. I've had to drop the life I've known (work in some capacity, exercise, travel, hanging with friends, activities) to meet the demands of managing this illness. In spite of this, I've been managing as well as I can, and I am so grateful for my friend Geneva's unwavering in-home support through all of it. I'm also fortunate to have been able to continue to fund my living expenses thus far, but the need for transitioning into work isn't far off. I sincerely believe I will be a more functional version of myself within a few months — but until then, I'm taking it one day at a time.
